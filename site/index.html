Our Story

Three Decades of Printing Excellence

Since 1994, Lino Press has been the beating heart of print in Upper Manhattan. Founded by Franklin Nuñez in Washington Heights, what started as a small community print shop has grown into New York City's first and largest Latino-owned printing company.

Today, our team — led by Franklin, his son Hector, and our talented designers — delivers everything from business cards to building-sized banners. We combine state-of-the-art digital and offset presses with old-school craftsmanship and the personal touch that only a family business can offer.

As a Union Printer and MBE-Certified firm, we're proud to serve government agencies, labor unions, nonprofit organizations, small businesses, and the vibrant communities of New York City — in English and Spanish.

FAQ

Common Questions

Do you offer same-day or rush printing?
Yes! We offer 24-hour rush printing for most products. Contact us for availability and rush pricing.
Are you a union printer?
Absolutely. Lino Press is a certified union shop. All our work carries the union bug, making us the right choice for labor organizations, political campaigns, and government contracts.
Do you provide bilingual design services?
Yes. Our design team creates materials in English, Spanish, or both. We understand the nuances of bilingual layouts and culturally-informed design.
What is MBE certification?
MBE stands for Minority Business Enterprise. Our certification qualifies us for city, state, and federal procurement contracts with minority business requirements.
Do you handle large-format and signage installation?
Yes. From design through production to installation, we handle banners, storefront signs, vehicle wraps, trade show displays, and more.
What file formats do you accept?
We accept PDF, AI, PSD, INDD, EPS, JPG, PNG, and TIFF. Our design team can also work from rough concepts or Word documents.
